The Problem

Three things that kill a case
before it starts.

01
Research Hell

Lawyers spend 60% of prep time on research. The other 40% on worrying about the research.

4.5 million judgments on Indian Kanoon. None of them organised for you. A senior advocate spends more time Ctrl+F-ing PDFs than actually building the argument. The backlog isn't just in courts — it's in preparation.

→ LexForge searches 4.5M judgments. Returns cited answers, not links.
02
The Hallucination Problem

ChatGPT cited a Supreme Court judge who doesn't exist. He was thrilled.

Lawyers have already been sanctioned internationally for citing AI-fabricated cases in actual filings. One bad citation = destroyed credibility = mistrial potential. LexForge only cites what it can retrieve. When it can't find something, it says so. Radical honesty.

→ Every citation flagged. Every source linked. "No direct authority" is a valid answer.
03
The Privacy Disaster

Attorney-client privilege. ChatGPT's terms of service. Pick one.

Uploading your client's matter to any cloud AI is a Bar Council violation waiting to be discovered. LexForge runs on your machine via Ollama. The model doesn't phone home. Your client's case stays in your office — not in a server farm in Oregon.

→ Local qwen3:8b. ChromaDB. Air-gapped if needed.
The Four Instruments

From first research
to final argument.

01
R

Research Mode

Search uploaded judgments and live cases from Indian Kanoon, SCI, and eCourts simultaneously. Binding vs persuasive authority — separated, not shuffled. A cited answer at 2am, not a list of links.

02

Argument Builder

Enter your case facts in plain language. LexForge identifies legal issues, retrieves authorities, and generates a complete IRAC brief — fully cited, with adverse cases distinguished — in under ten minutes.

IRAC · Auto-cited · Section-mapped · Fast

D

Devil's Advocate

The main act. LexForge plays opposing senior counsel AND simulates bench questions from five judge personas. It tears your argument apart — logically, sometimes brutally, occasionally with a sense of humour — so the other side can't.

Adversarial · Bench simulation · Priority actions · Local AI
04
M

Moot Court

Full adversarial simulation: Opening Submissions, Rebuttal, Sur-Rebuttal, Judicial Observations. Walk into the courtroom having already lost twice and rebuilt. The third time you walk in confident.

2 rounds · 5 bench personas · Judicial observations
Under the Hood

Technical credibility
matters in court.

qwen3:8b via Ollama
Local reasoning LLM. Runs on-device. No API keys, no data leaves the machine.
ChromaDB + nomic-embed-text
Vector store for uploaded judgments. Hybrid legal-structure chunking. Relevance scoring visible to the advocate — nothing hidden.
Indian Kanoon + SCI + eCourts
Live precedent search across millions of judgments. Five-source retrieval stack. Constitutional index for instant provision lookup.
FastAPI + SQLite
Research results and simulations auto-saved. Session history under briefs. PDF export. Runs locally on port 8000.
RAG with Citation Discipline
Every proposition must cite [SOURCE N]. Model instructed to write "No direct authority found" rather than fabricate. Honesty as a feature.
Five Judge Personas
Strict Proceduralist, Rights-Oriented, Commercial, Criminal, Constitutional. Each changes how arguments are attacked and what bench questions surface.
